Our Story

At Example Church, you will find a community gathered around one purpose — the Word of God. We read it, sing it, and teach it together every week. We exist to lead every generation to know Jesus and follow Jesus. Whether you are visiting for the first time or have been with us for years, you are welcome here.

Our Foundation

What We Believe

The Bible

The Holy Scriptures are the inspired, inerrant Word of God — the final and supreme authority for everything we believe, teach, and practice as followers of Christ.

2 Timothy 3:16–17 · 2 Peter 1:20–21 · Psalm 119:105 Read more ›

The Trinity

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three distinct Persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. These three are one in essence, power, and glory — equal in every divine perfection — yet distinct in their personal properties and relations.

Matthew 28:19 · 2 Corinthians 13:14 · Deuteronomy 6:4 Read more ›

Salvation

Salvation is by Grace alone, through Faith alone, in Jesus Christ alone. It is not earned by any effort or merit of our own, but freely given to all who trust in Him.

Ephesians 2:8–9 · Romans 5:1 · Acts 4:12 · Romans 3:28 Read more ›

The Church

The Church is the body of Christ, composed of all true believers — gathered to worship God, proclaim the gospel, and faithfully administer the Word and ordinances together.

Matthew 16:18 · Acts 2:41–42 · Hebrews 10:24–25 · Ephesians 4:11–12 Read more ›

Ordinances

We observe two ordinances given by Christ — Believer's Baptism by full immersion as a public declaration of Faith, and the Lord's Supper, a remembrance of His Sacrificial Death until He Returns.

Matthew 28:19 · Romans 6:3–4 · 1 Corinthians 11:23–26 Read more ›

Missions

The Lord Jesus calls every believer to make disciples of all nations. We take that seriously — beginning right here in our community and reaching outward to the ends of the earth.

Matthew 28:18–20 · Acts 1:8 · Romans 10:14–15 · Revelation 7:9–10 Read more ›

These are the convictions that shape everything we do. For our full statement of faith, or to read the Baptist Faith & Message 2000 that we affirm, visit the links below.

Curious?

Maybe you're not sure what you believe yet. Maybe someone invited you and you're on the fence. Maybe life has gotten hard and you're looking for something more. Wherever you're coming from — you're welcome here, exactly as you are.

01

Just show up

You don't need to have it all figured out. You don't need to dress a certain way or know all the right words. Come as you are — we mean that. Our doors open every Sunday at 9:15 AM, with worship beginning at 10:30 AM. Someone will greet you at the door, and if you have kids, we'll point you in the right direction.

02

See what it's like

A typical Sunday includes music, prayer, and an expository message — a careful, verse-by-verse walk through the Bible that's honest and practical. Services run about an hour and a half. Guests are never expected to give during the offering, and you're never put on the spot. No obligations — just come and see.

03

Talk to someone

If you have questions — about faith, about the Bible, about any of it — we'd love to have that conversation. Reach out anytime and we'll connect you with someone who's happy to talk, no strings attached.
