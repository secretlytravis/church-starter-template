The Gospel

The Romans Road

The Bible lays out a clear path to understanding who God is, who we are, and what He has done for us. Walk through it here — plainly and honestly.

1

We all have a problem.

The Bible is clear that every single person — no exceptions — has sinned against God. Sin is not just doing bad things. It is falling short of who God is and what He requires. And we all fall short.

Romans 3:23

"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."

This isn't meant to shame us. It's meant to be honest. Before we can understand the good news, we have to understand the problem.

Romans 3:10–12

"None is righteous, no, not one; no one understands; no one seeks for God. All have turned aside; together they have become worthless; no one does good, not even one."

This is not just about the worst people in history. Paul is writing about all of us — the religious, the moral, the well-meaning. By God's perfect standard, every human being has turned aside. We are not basically good people who occasionally make mistakes. We are sinners in need of rescue.

2

Sin has a consequence.

Because God is perfectly holy and just, sin cannot go unanswered. The consequence of sin is death — not just physical death, but eternal separation from God. This is the weight of what we're dealing with.

Romans 6:23a

"For the wages of sin is death..."

A wage is something that is earned. Our sin earns us death. That is sobering — but the verse doesn't end there.

Romans 1:18

"For the wrath of God is revealed from heaven against all ungodliness and unrighteousness of men, who by their unrighteousness suppress the truth."

God's wrath is not a temper tantrum — it is His perfect, settled opposition to everything that is evil and unjust. He cannot simply look the other way at sin any more than a righteous judge can dismiss a proven crime. God's Justice requires a response. He has to judge sin. The question is: who can bear it?

3

But God loves us.

Here is where everything changes. Even while we were still sinners — still living in rebellion against Him — God acted. God knew we couldn't clean ourselves up so He came to us.

Romans 5:8

"But God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."

Jesus Christ — fully God, fully man — lived the perfect life we could never live, and died the death we deserved. He took the penalty for our sin upon Himself so that we wouldn't have to bear it.

Romans 5:6

"For while we were still weak, at the right time Christ died for the ungodly."

Notice the timing: at the right time. This was not an accident or a last resort. The death of Jesus on the cross was the eternal plan of God to rescue sinners — planned before the foundation of the world, carried out at exactly the moment God appointed. The cross is the greatest act of love in all of history, directed at people who did nothing to deserve it.

4

The gift is free.

Remember the verse that said the wages of sin is death? Here is how it ends:

Romans 6:23

"For the wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."

Eternal life cannot be earned. It cannot be bought. It cannot be achieved through religious effort or good behavior. It is a gift — freely given by God through Jesus Christ. You simply receive it.

Romans 5:1

"Therefore, since we have been justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ."

Justified means declared righteous — not because we are, but because Christ is, and His righteousness is credited to us by faith. The result is peace with God. Not just a feeling of peace, but an actual change in our standing before Him. We go from enemies to sons and daughters. That is what the free gift produces.

5

How do we receive it?

The gift is received through faith — by confessing that Jesus is Lord and believing in your heart that God raised Him from the dead. This is not a formula. It is a genuine turning of your heart to God.

Romans 10:9

"Because, if you confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved."

To confess that Jesus is Lord is to acknowledge that He has authority over your life — that you are surrendering to Him, not just adding Him to your existing plans. To believe in Jesus' resurrection is to trust that His death actually accomplished something: that sin and death have been defeated, and that He is alive today.

Romans 10:11

"For the Scripture says, 'Everyone who believes in him will not be put to shame.'"

Romans 10:13

"For everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved."

Everyone. That word means you. Whatever you've done. Wherever you've been. God will not turn away anyone who comes to Him in genuine faith. You will not be put to shame. If you call on Him, He will save you — not because you are worthy, but because He is faithful to His promise.

If You're Ready

A Prayer of Faith

There are no magic words that save you — God looks at the heart. But if you want to express your faith to Him right now, here is a simple prayer. Pray it honestly, from your heart, and mean every word.

"Lord Jesus, I know that I am a sinner and that I deserve death. But I believe that You died for my sins, that You were buried, and that You rose again. I turn from my sin and I place my trust in You alone as my Lord and Savior. Save me, Lord — not because I deserve it, but because You promised that everyone who calls on Your name will be saved. Thank You for the gift of eternal life. Amen."

If you prayed that prayer and meant it — welcome to the family of God. This is the most important decision you will ever make, and we would love to walk with you from here. Please let us know.

Before You Go

Common Questions

What about people who have never heard the gospel?

This is an honest question and the Bible takes it seriously. Romans 1:20 tells us God has made His existence plain through creation, so all people are without excuse. What we can say with full confidence is that God is perfectly just — He will not condemn anyone unfairly. The more pressing question, though, is what you will do now that you have heard.

What if I don't feel any different?

Salvation is a fact, not a feeling. If you have genuinely confessed Jesus as Lord and believed in His resurrection, Scripture says you are saved — God's promise does not depend on your emotions. Many new believers feel little at first, and that is not a sign something went wrong. If you are genuinely uncertain, talk to a pastor. That conversation is never a waste of time.

What if I sin again after trusting Christ?

You will — every believer does. The Christian life is not sinless perfection, it is an ongoing battle against sin with the help of the Holy Spirit. Your salvation is not undone every time you fall. Confess it, receive the forgiveness that is already yours in Christ, and press forward.

1 John 1:9

"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness."

Do I have to clean up my life before I come to God?

No — and you cannot. That is the whole point of the gospel. You come to God exactly as you are, and He receives you not because of what you bring, but because of what Christ has done. The transformation comes after salvation, not before it. He does not require you to be better first. He requires you to stop trusting in yourself and start trusting in His Son.

I still have questions. What do I do?

Ask them. Honest questions are a sign of a seeking heart, and God honors that. Reach out to us and we will point you to what the Bible says — no pressure, no judgment.
