First Time Visiting?

Plan Your Visit

Walking into a new church can feel like a big step. We would be honored to welcome you, help you find your way, and give you room to settle in at your own pace.

Service Times Let Us Know
When We Gather

You Are Welcome Here

Sunday School

9:15 AM
A relaxed time to sit with a smaller group, open the Bible together, ask honest questions, and begin getting to know people before worship. If you are new, this can be one of the easiest places to meet someone.

Sunday Worship

10:30 AM
Our main weekly gathering. We sing, pray, read Scripture, and hear the Bible preached in a way that aims to be faithful, clear, and helpful for real life.

Wednesday Bible Study

6:00 PM
A quieter midweek gathering for prayer, Bible study, and encouragement. It is a good place to listen, learn, and be known over time.

What to Expect

A Normal Sunday

Come as you are

You do not need to dress a certain way or know church language. Some people dress casually, others dress up. Come in whatever helps you show up.

You can take your time

We are glad to meet guests, but we will not single you out or pressure you to give during the offering. You are free to observe, listen, and ask questions when you are ready.

The Bible will be central

The sermon is usually a careful walk through a passage of Scripture, with the goal of understanding God's Word and applying it honestly.

Children are welcome

If you have children, bring them with you. Someone will be glad to help you find the right place, answer questions, and make the morning feel less confusing.

Location: 123 Church Street, Your City, ST 00000. If you are unsure where to go when you arrive, look for someone near the entrance. We will be glad to help you get settled.

Let Us Know

Planning to Visit?

This is completely optional. If it would make your first visit easier, let us know you are coming or send us a question. We would be glad to help before you ever walk through the door.

Have a question before Sunday?

We would be glad to help. Send a note, call the church office, or simply come Sunday morning. There is no pressure either way.
