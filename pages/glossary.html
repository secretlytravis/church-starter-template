First-Time Visitor Guide

Church Glossary

If you are new to church, some words may sound familiar but still feel unclear. Here are simple explanations of common terms you may hear on Sunday.

You do not need to know all of these words before you visit. This page is simply here to take away some of the mystery, so you can listen, ask questions, and feel a little more at home.

Start Here

Here Are the Basics

If you only read one part of this page before visiting, start here. These are the words you are most likely to hear in a normal worship service.

Term Plain Meaning Why It Matters
Gospel The good news that Jesus died for sinners, rose from the dead, and saves everyone who trusts Him. This is the center of Christian faith and the message every sermon ultimately points back to.
Grace God's undeserved kindness toward people who cannot save themselves. Christians believe salvation is a gift from God, not something earned by being religious or good enough.
Faith Trusting God and relying on Jesus Christ. Faith is how a person receives Christ, rests in His work, and begins following Him.
Scripture Another word for the Bible, understood as God's written Word. The Bible is the church's final authority for what we believe, teach, and practice.
Sermon The main Bible message during worship. At Example Church, preaching usually explains a passage carefully and applies it to everyday life.
Prayer Speaking with God through praise, confession, thanks, and requests. Prayer is how the church depends on God together, not a performance for people in the room.
Worship Honoring God with our whole lives; in church, this includes singing, prayer, Scripture, and preaching. Worship is not only music. The whole gathering is meant to turn our attention to God.
Baptism A public act where a believer is placed under water and raised up again. It shows faith in Jesus' death and resurrection and publicly identifies someone as His follower.
Communion Also called the Lord's Supper, using bread and juice to remember Jesus' sacrifice. It helps believers remember the cross, examine their hearts, and proclaim Christ's death until He returns.
Pastor A shepherd-like leader who teaches Scripture and cares for the church. Pastors are not spiritual celebrities; they are called to serve, teach, pray, and help guide God's people.

Showing all terms.

Amen

A word that means "truly" or "let it be so." People may say it after prayer or in agreement with biblical truth.

Apologetics

Thoughtful answers to honest questions about Christianity, the Bible, Jesus, and why Christians believe the gospel is true.

Apostle

A messenger sent by Jesus. The apostles were early eyewitness leaders who helped lay the foundation of the church.

Atonement

What Jesus accomplished by dying for sinners. He took the penalty for sin so people could be forgiven and brought to God.

Baptism

A public act where a believer is placed under water and raised up again, showing faith in Jesus' death and resurrection.

Benediction

A short blessing, usually from Scripture, spoken near the end of a worship service before the church is dismissed.

Bible Study

A smaller gathering where people read, discuss, and apply Scripture together, often with more conversation than a Sunday sermon.

Born Again

Jesus' phrase for the new spiritual life God gives a person who trusts in Him.

Call to Worship

A Scripture reading or spoken invitation near the beginning of a service that calls the church to focus on God.

Canon

The recognized books of the Bible. Christians believe these writings are God's inspired Word.

Chapter and Verse

The numbering system used to help people find a specific place in the Bible, like John 3:16.

Church Covenant

A shared promise among church members about how they will worship, serve, love, and care for one another.

Church Member

Someone who has formally joined a local church and committed to worship, serve, give, and grow with that congregation.

Communion

Also called the Lord's Supper. Believers share bread and juice to remember Jesus' sacrifice and proclaim His death.

Confession

Honestly admitting sin to God and trusting His mercy, rather than hiding, excusing, or pretending.

Congregation

The gathered people of the church. It simply means the members and visitors worshiping together in one place.

Conversion

The turning point when a person turns from sin and trusts in Jesus Christ for salvation.

Covenant

A serious promise or relationship commitment. The Bible often uses this word for God's gracious promises to His people.

Creed

A short statement of core Christian belief, often used by churches to summarize what Christians have confessed for centuries.

Cross

The place where Jesus died. Christians often use this word to speak of His sacrifice for sinners.

Deacon

A servant leader in the church who helps care for practical needs and supports the ministry of the congregation.

Devotion

Personal time set aside for prayer, Bible reading, and worship, often during the week outside of church gatherings.

Disciple

A follower and learner of Jesus. Christians are called disciples because they trust Christ and learn to walk in His ways.

Discipleship

The lifelong process of following Jesus, growing in faith, and helping others follow Him too.

Doctrine

Christian teaching drawn from the Bible. Doctrine explains what the church believes about God, salvation, Scripture, and life.

Doxology

A short expression of praise to God, often sung or spoken, especially after reflecting on His greatness and grace.

Elder

A pastoral leader who helps shepherd, teach, and oversee the church. Some churches use this word alongside pastor.

Epistle

A letter in the New Testament, written to churches or individuals to teach, encourage, correct, and strengthen faith.

Eternal Life

The life God gives through Jesus. It begins now by knowing Him and continues forever with Him.

Evangelism

Telling others the good news of Jesus Christ and inviting them to turn from sin and trust Him.

Expository Preaching

Preaching that explains and applies the meaning of a Bible passage, usually working through Scripture verse by verse.

Faith

Trusting God and relying on Jesus Christ. Faith is more than knowing facts; it is resting your life on Him.

Fellowship

Christian friendship and shared life. It can include meals, conversation, encouragement, prayer, and caring for one another.

Forgiveness

God's pardon of sin because of Jesus. Christians also seek to forgive others because God has forgiven them.

Glory

The beauty, greatness, and worth of God. To glorify God means to honor Him as He deserves.

Gospel

The good news that Jesus died for sinners, rose from the dead, and saves everyone who turns to Him in faith.

Grace

God's undeserved kindness. We are saved by grace, not because we earned God's love or performed well enough.

Great Commission

Jesus' command to make disciples of all nations by going, baptizing, and teaching people to obey Him.

Heaven

The place of God's presence and the future home of all who belong to Christ.

Holiness

God's perfect purity and otherness. For Christians, holiness also means being set apart to live for Him.

Holy Spirit

The third Person of the Trinity, who gives new life, comforts, convicts, teaches, and helps believers follow Jesus.

Hymn

A song of worship, often rich in biblical truth, sung by the congregation to praise God and encourage faith.

Imago Dei

A Latin phrase meaning "image of God." It means every person has dignity and worth because God made them.

Incarnation

The truth that the Son of God became truly human in Jesus Christ while remaining truly God.

Inerrancy

The belief that the Bible, as God gave it, is true and without error in all it teaches.

Intercession

Praying for someone else. When the church prays for needs, that is a form of intercession.

Invitation

A moment near the end of some services when people are invited to respond, pray, ask questions, or speak with a pastor.

Justification

God declaring a sinner right with Him because of Jesus Christ, received by faith and not earned by good works.

Kingdom of God

God's reign and rule. Christians pray for His kingdom to be seen as people trust and obey Him.

Lament

A prayer or song that honestly brings grief, pain, or confusion to God while still trusting Him.

Lord

A title for Jesus that means He has authority. Christians confess Jesus as Lord, not merely as a teacher.

Lord's Day

A Christian name for Sunday, the day believers gather to worship because Jesus rose from the dead on the first day.

Lord's Supper

Another name for communion, where believers remember Jesus' body given and blood shed for them.

Mercy

God's compassion toward people in need and His kindness in not giving sinners what their sin deserves.

Missionary

Someone sent to share the gospel and serve others, often in another community, culture, or country.

Missions

The work of making Christ known beyond the church, locally and globally, through evangelism, service, and church planting.

New Testament

The part of the Bible that tells of Jesus' life, death, resurrection, and the early church.

Old Testament

The first part of the Bible, written before Jesus' birth, preparing for and pointing forward to Him.

Offering

Money given by church members to support ministry, missions, and practical needs. Guests are not expected to give.

Ordinance

A practice Jesus gave the church to observe. Baptists usually use this word for baptism and the Lord's Supper.

Pastor

A shepherd-like leader who teaches Scripture, cares for the church, prays for people, and helps guide the congregation.

Pastoral Care

The spiritual care pastors and church leaders give through prayer, counsel, visits, teaching, and encouragement.

Prayer

Speaking with God. Prayer can include praise, confession, thanks, requests, and quiet dependence on Him.

Prayer Request

Something you ask the church or pastor to pray about, such as a need, burden, illness, decision, or thanksgiving.

Providence

God's wise care and rule over all things, even when people cannot see what He is doing.

Pulpit

The stand or place where the Bible is read and preached. The word can also refer to the preaching ministry itself.

Redemption

God rescuing people from sin through Jesus, like buying someone out of slavery and bringing them home.

Repentance

Turning from sin toward God. Repentance is not just feeling bad; it is a changed direction of heart and life.

Resurrection

Jesus rising bodily from the dead. Christians also believe all people will one day be raised.

Reverence

Deep respect and holy awe toward God. Reverence does not remove joy, but it keeps worship from becoming careless.

Salvation

God's rescue of sinners through Jesus Christ. It includes forgiveness, new life, and being made right with God.

Sanctification

The lifelong work of God making believers more like Jesus in their thoughts, desires, words, and actions.

Scripture

Another name for the Bible, understood by Christians to be God's written Word and our final authority.

Sermon

The main Bible message in a worship service, where a pastor explains Scripture and applies it to everyday life.

Sin

Anything in us that disobeys, rejects, or falls short of God's holy character and commands.

Small Group

A smaller gathering for Bible study, prayer, encouragement, and relationships, often meeting outside the main worship service.

Sovereignty

God's complete rule and authority over all things. Nothing is outside His power, wisdom, or care.

Statement of Faith

A written summary of what a church believes the Bible teaches about major Christian doctrines.

Stewardship

Using what God has entrusted to us, such as time, money, gifts, and opportunities, for His purposes.

Sunday School

A smaller Sunday morning class for learning the Bible, asking questions, and building relationships before worship.

Testimony

A personal account of what God has done in someone's life, especially how they came to faith in Jesus.

Theology

The study of God and what He has revealed about Himself in Scripture.

Trinity

The Christian belief that there is one God who eternally exists as Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Usher

A church volunteer who helps people find seats, receive information, and move comfortably through the service.

VBS

Short for Vacation Bible School, a children's ministry event with Bible teaching, songs, crafts, and activities.

Verse

A numbered sentence or section in the Bible that helps people find and refer to a specific passage.

Worship

Honoring God with our whole lives. In church, it often includes singing, prayer, Scripture, giving, and preaching.

Worship Service

The main gathering of the church for singing, praying, reading Scripture, giving, and hearing the Bible preached.
