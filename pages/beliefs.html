Our Foundation

What We Believe

These are the convictions that shape everything we do — how we worship, how we preach, how we serve, and how we live together as a church family.

The Scriptures· The Godhead· Man & Sin· Salvation· The Church· The Christian Life· Last Things & Missions

I

The Scriptures

Inspiration & Inerrancy

The Bible is the inspired, inerrant Word of God — breathed out by the Holy Spirit through human authors. It is without error in its original manuscripts and is fully trustworthy in all that it affirms.

2 Timothy 3:16–17 · 2 Peter 1:20–21

Authority

Scripture is the supreme and final authority for all matters of faith and practice. No tradition, experience, or human reason may override what God has spoken in His Word.

Psalm 119:105 · Isaiah 8:20 · John 17:17

Sufficiency

The Bible contains everything necessary for salvation and godly living. It equips the believer thoroughly for every good work God calls them to.

2 Timothy 3:17 · Psalm 19:7–11 · Deuteronomy 29:29

II

The Godhead

The Trinity

There is one living and true God, eternally existing in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — each fully God, equal in power and glory, distinct in person and role.

Deuteronomy 6:4 · Matthew 28:19 · 2 Corinthians 13:14

God the Father

God the Father reigns with sovereign authority over all creation. He ordains all things for His glory and accomplishes redemption through His Son according to His eternal purpose.

Isaiah 46:9–10 · Ephesians 1:3–6 · John 6:37–40

God the Son

Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God, fully God and fully man. He lived without sin, died as our substitute, rose bodily from the grave, and will return in glory to reign forever.

John 1:1–14 · Hebrews 4:15 · 1 Corinthians 15:3–4 · Acts 1:11

God the Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit is fully God — He convicts, regenerates, indwells, and sanctifies every believer. He applies the work of Christ and seals God's people until the day of redemption.

John 16:8–11 · Romans 8:11 · Ephesians 1:13–14 · Titus 3:5

III

Man & Sin

Creation in God's Image

God created humanity — male and female — in His own image, with dignity, worth, and purpose. Every human life bears the imprint of its Creator and is therefore sacred.

Genesis 1:26–27 · Psalm 139:13–16 · Acts 17:28

The Fall

Through Adam's sin, all of humanity fell. Sin entered the world, bringing death, corruption, and a broken relationship with God that we are powerless to repair on our own.

Genesis 3:1–19 · Romans 5:12 · Romans 3:23

Total Depravity

Every part of human nature — mind, will, and affection — is corrupted by sin. Apart from God's Grace, no one seeks Him, and no one can come to Him on their own.

Romans 3:10–12 · Ephesians 2:1–3 · Jeremiah 17:9

IV

Salvation

Grace Alone

Salvation originates entirely in God's Grace. It is not owed to us and cannot be earned. God, in His mercy, reaches down to those who deserve only judgment.

Ephesians 2:8–9 · Titus 3:4–5 · Romans 3:24

Faith Alone

Salvation is received through Faith alone — not by works, ritual, or religious effort. We trust entirely in Christ and in nothing we bring to God ourselves.

Romans 3:28 · Romans 5:1 · Galatians 2:16

Christ Alone

Jesus Christ is the only way of salvation. There is no other name under heaven by which we must be saved — no other mediator, no other sacrifice, no other hope.

Acts 4:12 · John 14:6 · 1 Timothy 2:5

Regeneration

The new birth is a sovereign work of the Holy Spirit, not a decision of human will. God transforms the heart, giving the spiritually dead new life and the desire to follow Christ.

John 3:3–8 · Titus 3:5 · Ezekiel 36:26–27

Justification

God declares the believing sinner righteous on the basis of Christ's perfect obedience and atoning death. Our sins are forgiven and Christ's righteousness is credited to us.

Romans 5:1 · Romans 4:5 · 2 Corinthians 5:21

Sanctification & Glorification

Every believer is progressively conformed to the image of Christ by the Holy Spirit. This work will be completed at glorification — when we see Him face to face.

Romans 8:29–30 · Philippians 1:6 · 1 John 3:2

V

The Church

The Body of Christ

The Church is the body of Christ — all true believers united to Him and to one another. The local church is God's appointed means for worship, fellowship, discipleship, and mission.

Matthew 16:18 · Ephesians 1:22–23 · Acts 2:41–47

Church Membership

Membership in the local church is a meaningful covenant commitment — to gather, to serve, to give, and to hold one another accountable to the Word of God.

Hebrews 10:24–25 · Acts 2:42 · 1 Corinthians 12:12–27

Baptism

Believer's Baptism by full immersion is the public declaration of a person's Faith in Christ — an outward picture of an inward reality. It follows salvation and precedes church membership.

Matthew 28:19 · Romans 6:3–4 · Acts 8:36–38

The Lord's Supper

The Lord's Supper is a remembrance of Christ's Sacrificial Death, observed regularly by the church until He returns. It proclaims His death and anticipates His coming.

1 Corinthians 11:23–26 · Luke 22:19–20 · Matthew 26:26–29

Church Leadership

The New Testament establishes two offices — pastor and deacon. Pastors lead, teach, and shepherd the flock. Deacons serve the practical needs of the body.

1 Timothy 3:1–13 · Titus 1:5–9 · Acts 6:1–6

Church Discipline

The church is called to lovingly confront unrepentant sin among its members — for the good of the individual, the purity of the body, and the honor of Christ's name.

Matthew 18:15–20 · 1 Corinthians 5:1–13 · Galatians 6:1

VI

The Christian Life

Prayer

Prayer is the ongoing communion of the believer with God — bringing needs, praise, and confession before Him in confidence that He hears and answers according to His will.

Philippians 4:6–7 · 1 Thessalonians 5:17 · Matthew 6:9–13

The Lord's Day

Sunday — the first day of the week — is set apart for worship, rest, and the gathering of the church. It commemorates Christ's resurrection and anticipates His eternal rest.

Acts 20:7 · Revelation 1:10 · Hebrews 10:25

Stewardship

Everything we have belongs to God. We are stewards — not owners — of our time, talents, and treasure, and we are called to give generously and faithfully to His work.

Malachi 3:10 · 2 Corinthians 9:6–8 · Luke 16:10–12

Marriage & Family

Marriage is a covenant between one man and one woman, ordained by God to reflect Christ's love for His Church. The family is the foundational unit of society and the primary place of discipleship.

Genesis 2:24 · Matthew 19:4–6 · Ephesians 5:22–33

VII

Last Things & Missions

The Return of Christ

Jesus Christ will return bodily and visibly to this earth in glory. His return is certain, personal, and imminent — the blessed hope of every believer.

Acts 1:11 · 1 Thessalonians 4:16–17 · Titus 2:13

Resurrection & Judgment

All people will be raised — believers to eternal life and unbelievers to eternal judgment. God's justice will be perfectly and finally satisfied on that day.

John 5:28–29 · Revelation 20:11–15 · 2 Corinthians 5:10

Heaven

Those who trust in Christ will spend eternity with God — freed from sin, sorrow, and death forever. Heaven is not merely a place but the perfect, unhindered presence of God.

John 14:1–3 · Revelation 21:1–4 · 1 Corinthians 2:9

Hell

Those who reject Christ will face eternal separation from God in conscious judgment. Hell is real, just, and a sobering motivation for the urgency of the gospel.

Matthew 25:41–46 · Revelation 20:14–15 · 2 Thessalonians 1:9

Evangelism & Missions

Every believer is called to share the gospel. The Great Commission is not optional — it is the marching order of the church until Christ returns.

Matthew 28:18–20 · Acts 1:8 · Romans 10:14–15

The Nations

God's redemptive purpose spans every tribe, tongue, and nation. We long for the day when people from every corner of the earth stand before the throne of the Lamb.

Revelation 7:9–10 · Genesis 12:1–3 · Isaiah 49:6

We affirm the Baptist Faith & Message 2000 as a faithful summary of biblical doctrine and a shared confession with our brothers and sisters across the Southern Baptist Convention.
