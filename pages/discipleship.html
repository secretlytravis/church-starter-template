Growing Together

Biblical Discipleship

Discipleship is not a program. It is one believer walking alongside another — in the Word, in prayer, and in life — so that both grow more like Christ.

The Foundation

What Is Biblical Discipleship?

The word disciple simply means a learner — someone who follows a teacher so closely that they begin to think, speak, and live the way their teacher does. Jesus called twelve men to follow Him in exactly this way, and before He ascended, He commanded His followers to go and do the same thing with everyone, everywhere.

Matthew 28:19–20

"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you."

Discipleship is not a class you attend or a curriculum you complete. It is a relationship — intentional, ongoing, and built around the Word of God. It is one person who knows Christ a little better helping another person know Him more. It happens over coffee, in Scripture, on the phone, and in the mess of everyday life.

This is how the faith has always been passed on. Not primarily through institutions or programs, but through faithful men and women pouring what they have received into the people around them — and those people doing the same.

2 Timothy 2:2

"And what you have heard from me in the presence of many witnesses entrust to faithful men, who will be able to teach others also."

Paul's vision here is a chain — Timothy receives from Paul, passes it to faithful people, who pass it to others. Four generations of discipleship in one verse. That is the pattern God designed for the health and growth of His church.

Luke 6:40

"A disciple is not above his teacher, but everyone when he is fully trained will be like his teacher."

The goal of discipleship is not knowledge — it is transformation. Jesus is not producing students who can pass a theology exam. He is producing people who are being conformed to His image. That is the measure of biblical discipleship: not what a person knows, but who they are becoming.

Romans 8:29

"For those whom he foreknew he also predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son, in order that he might be the firstborn among many brothers."

God's ultimate purpose for every believer is conformity to Christ. Discipleship is the primary human means through which that transformation happens — as one person who is being changed helps another to be changed by the same grace and the same Spirit.

Why It Matters

You Were Not Meant to Grow Alone

The Christian life was never designed to be a solo endeavor. The New Testament assumes that believers are embedded in community — knowing one another, accountable to one another, serving one another, and spurring one another on. Discipleship is the intentional, personal expression of that community.

Without discipleship, believers drift. They may attend church faithfully and still have no one who knows the state of their soul — no one asking the hard questions, no one opening the Word with them, no one to call when things fall apart. The church was designed to prevent exactly that.

Discipleship is also how the church grows — not just in numbers, but in depth. A church full of deeply rooted disciples is a church that can weather anything. A church full of spectators is fragile. The difference is made one relationship at a time.

Ephesians 4:11–13

"And he gave the apostles, the prophets, the evangelists, the shepherds and teachers, to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ, until we all attain to the unity of the faith and of the knowledge of the Son of God, to mature manhood, to the measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ."

God gave the church pastors and teachers not to do all the ministry themselves, but to equip every member to do it. The body builds itself up in love as each part does its work. Discipleship is how that equipping happens — one person at a time, in the context of real relationship.

Hebrews 10:24–25

"And let us consider how to stir up one another to love and good works, not neglecting to meet together, as is the habit of some, but encouraging one another, and all the more as you see the Day drawing near."

Notice the active language — stir up, encourage, consider how. This is not passive. Biblical community takes intention, and discipleship is the most intentional form of it.

Proverbs 27:17

"Iron sharpens iron, and one man sharpens another."

The image is striking — iron on iron, friction and force, producing something sharper than what came in. Growth is rarely comfortable. But the Christian who allows another believer to speak honestly into their life will be sharpened in ways that private devotion alone cannot produce.

The Model

How Jesus Made Disciples

Jesus did not run a program. He called twelve men to be with Him — to watch Him, listen to Him, ask Him questions, and follow His example. Over three years, through ordinary life and extraordinary circumstances, He shaped them into the people who would turn the world upside down.

Mark 3:14

"And he appointed twelve (whom he also named apostles) so that they might be with him and he might send them out to preach."

Two purposes: to be with Him, and to be sent out. Presence came before proclamation. Jesus did not send them out until they had been with Him. That order still matters. Discipleship is first about being with — being with Christ, being with a mature believer — before it is about being sent.

His method is still the best one. Biblical discipleship follows the same basic pattern:

01
Intentional relationship

Discipleship begins when someone decides to invest in another person on purpose — not waiting for it to happen accidentally. Jesus called people by name. He chose them. That intentionality matters.

02
The Word at the center

Everything flows from Scripture. The discipler opens the Bible with the one being discipled — reading it, studying it, applying it, memorizing it. The Word is not one ingredient among many. It is the foundation.

03
Accountability and prayer

Discipleship involves honest questions and honest answers. How is your walk with God? Where are you struggling? What are you believing that isn't true? And it is saturated in prayer — for and with one another.

04
Life on life

The best discipleship happens not just in a scheduled meeting but in the margins of life — the car ride, the meal, the phone call, the moment of crisis. Jesus lived with His disciples. We let people into our lives.

05
Multiplication in view

The goal is never just a better Christian. The goal is a disciple who makes disciples. From the beginning, discipleship has a horizon — the person you are pouring into will one day pour into someone else.

06
Rooted in the church

Discipleship does not replace the local church — it flourishes inside it. The gathering, the preaching, the ordinances, the community — all of it provides the soil in which discipleship takes root and grows.

At Example Church

How We Practice Discipleship

Acts 2:42

"And they devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching and the fellowship, to the breaking of bread and the prayers."

From the very first days of the church, believers gathered around the Word, around one another, and around prayer. That pattern has never changed. We encourage two complementary approaches to discipleship — one focused on smaller accountability groups, and one built around one-on-one investment. Neither is more biblical than the other. Both reflect how Jesus made disciples.

D-Groups

Small Group Discipleship

A D-Group (Discipleship Group) is a small, committed group of three to five same-gender believers who meet weekly to read Scripture together, memorize verses, pray for one another, and hold each other accountable to following Christ.

The small size is intentional — it creates the kind of trust and honesty that a large group rarely produces. Everyone is known. Everyone is accountable. Everyone is growing.

D-Groups are not a Bible study with an audience. They are a covenant — a commitment to show up, be honest, and press one another toward Christ week after week.

1-on-1

One-on-One Discipleship

One-on-one discipleship is the most personal form — one believer meeting regularly with another to open the Word, pray, and walk together through the Christian life. It is especially valuable for new believers and those going through significant seasons of growth or struggle.

This is the model Paul describes in 2 Timothy 2:2 — what you have received, pass on to someone faithful. It is slow, personal, and deeply effective.

If you are a new believer, or simply someone who has never had anyone invest in you this way — we want to help change that. Reach out and we will do our best to connect you with someone.

Getting Started

Where Do I Begin?

1 Thessalonians 2:8

"So, being affectionately desirous of you, we were ready to share with you not only the gospel of God but also our own selves, because you had become very dear to us."

Paul's model of discipleship was not a transfer of information — it was a sharing of life. He gave the Thessalonians not just the gospel but himself. That is the kind of investment biblical discipleship calls us to, and it begins with a decision to show up for someone.

The most common reason people are never discipled is that they are waiting for it to happen to them. Discipleship is almost always initiated — someone has to take a step. That step can feel awkward, but it is almost always worth it.

If you want to be discipled — ask someone. If you see someone newer in the faith than you — invest in them. If you do not know where to start — reach out to us and we will help you find your footing.

Discipleship is available to everyone at Example Church — men, women, new believers, and those who have walked with Christ for decades. You are never too young in the faith to be poured into, and never too mature to stop growing.

Colossians 1:28–29

"Him we proclaim, warning everyone and teaching everyone with all wisdom, that we may present everyone mature in Christ. For this I toil, struggling with all his energy that he powerfully works within me."

2 Peter 3:18

"But grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. To him be the glory both now and to the day of eternity. Amen."

The goal is maturity in Christ — for everyone. That is what we are after. Not a program, not a statistic, not a checkbox. Faithful men and women growing in the grace and knowledge of Jesus Christ, together.
