How to Make a Church Style Guide

Use this page as a working checklist for turning the starter design into a clear, consistent visual identity for a real church.

Purpose

What a Style Guide Does

A style guide gives volunteers and future editors one place to check before changing the site. It explains the colors, fonts, images, buttons, tone, page structure, and common rules that keep the website from feeling patched together over time.

For a church website, the goal is not to sound corporate or overly polished. The goal is clarity, trust, warmth, and consistency.

Step 1

Write Down the Church Identity

Begin with plain answers before choosing colors or layouts. A good design flows from who the church is and who the site is serving.

  • Church name and short name
  • City and community served
  • Primary audience: first-time guests, members, families, seekers, or all of the above
  • Three words that describe the desired feel, such as warm, reverent, simple, joyful, historic, or modern
  • One sentence that explains the church mission

Tip: If the church already has printed bulletins, signage, or a logo, use those as clues. A website should feel like the same church people visit on Sunday.

Step 2

Choose a Color Palette

Pick a small set of colors and use them everywhere. Most church websites only need a dark anchor color, one accent color, a light background, and readable text colors.

Primary #18362f

Used for navigation, dark sections, headings, and strong visual anchors.

Secondary #2f5d50

Used for hover states, secondary panels, and quiet supporting elements.

Accent #b86f3a

Used for buttons, borders, labels, and small moments of emphasis.

Background #f6f1e8

Used for warm page backgrounds that are easier on the eyes than pure white.

Update the color variables in each page's `:root` block and in `assets/css/site-enhancements.css` when changing the palette.

Step 3

Choose Fonts

This starter uses one serif font for headings and one sans-serif font for body copy. That is usually enough.

  • Heading font: should feel appropriate for worship, Scripture, and church history.
  • Body font: should be easy to read on phones.
  • Avoid using more than two font families unless there is a strong reason.
font-family: 'Playfair Display', serif;
font-family: 'Ubuntu', sans-serif;
Step 4

Define Image Rules

Write down what kind of photos belong on the site. This keeps the site from drifting into random stock imagery.

  • Use real photos of the building, worship space, people, staff, and community when possible.
  • Keep photos bright, clear, and welcoming.
  • Avoid images that are dark, blurry, overly filtered, or emotionally vague.
  • Always use meaningful `alt` text for important images.
Step 5

Document Buttons and Links

Decide which button style is used for primary actions and which is used for secondary actions. Keep the wording clear and visitor-focused.

  • Primary buttons: Plan Your Visit, Watch Sermons, Contact Us
  • Secondary buttons: Learn More, Back to Home, View Archive
  • Inline links: use them for supporting references, not major page actions
Step 6

Set the Writing Voice

For a church website, writing should usually be warm, plain, and direct. Avoid vague slogans that could belong to any organization.

  • Say what visitors need to know.
  • Use normal words before churchy words.
  • Explain theological words when they appear.
  • Keep page introductions short.
  • Use a hopeful tone without exaggerating.
Step 7

Record Page Patterns

List the page sections that should repeat across the site. This makes future pages easier to build.

Page header
Short introduction
Main content sections
Clear next step
Footer with contact information

When a new page is added, copy an existing page with a similar structure and then change the content.

Step 8

Keep a Maintenance Checklist

At the bottom of the style guide, keep a short checklist for future edits.

  • Does the page use the approved colors?
  • Are the fonts consistent?
  • Are images clear and properly described?
  • Does the page have one obvious next step?
  • Does it work on a phone?
  • Are contact details still correct?
